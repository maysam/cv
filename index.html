Working Experience

July 2013 - Present

Contracting Front End Developer

I joined a small team of developers four months after FutureLearn was founded to help build a Beta product fit for public unveiling in mid-Sept.

Within the first 3 months, the user base quickly grew to over 250,000 users. In Oct 2014, after just one year live, FutureLearn is fast approaching one million registered users.

The product is built in Ruby on Rails, using HAML and SASS for front end styling. The team worked using Agile to gain real-time feedback from beta users to shape the product. I was one of two contracting Front Enders who helped build a scalable, modular, front end architecture which the maturing team can now inherit and build upon further.

  • Worked with other developers to create a consistent Pattern Library.
  • Using Continuous deployment to deploy on average 5 times a day deploy on average.
  • FutureLearn was awarded 'Startup of the Year' at the BIMAS 2014.

June 2013 - July 2013

Senior Front End Developer

  • Responsible for Front End build of Paul Smith Fashion redesign on Drupal with Magento eCommerce integration.

July 2007 - June 2012

Client Side Developer / Web Developer

I worked at the BBC for just under 5 years in total, progressing from a Junior Client Side Developer at BBC Switch to spending my last eighteen months on the high profile BBC iPlayer team.

In this role, I worked within a large project team to deliver continuous improvements and features to the BBC's high profile TV & iPlayer website.

I worked in an Agile team of ~10 developers, and was required to produce high performing, well written, object oriented code on a daily basis.

Working closely with my Project Manager, designers, other developers and testers within two week release cycles, I help shape new features and improvements that enhanced the user experience for the millions of visitors that hit BBC iPlayer each week.

We were one of the first teams within the BBC to embrace responsive, launching the Channels (www.bbc.co.uk/tv) websites just before I left in June 2012, which went on to be the foundation for the revamped iPlayer site as it is today.

  • In this time, I spent eighteen months on attachment to BBC Northern Ireland
  • In BBCNI, I had responsibility of supporting a large portfolio of legacy sites within a small technical team in Belfast.
  • Involved in producing live events sites for BBC Radio 1
  • Worked on high visibility products such as Human Planet, Battle of Britain and the Royal Wedding.
